Mosaic Product Design Internship


Mosaic is a Series B, tech-enabled general contractor for the residential development industry merging technology with construction services. I wore multiple hats within the software side over the course of my internship: conducting interviews, writing product documents, and making design iterations for a quick turnaround following feedback from users. Primarily, I worked on two new features for our company's construction management software.

My main project was around understanding expectations our partners in the construction industry have for the technology we build and needfinding areas of improvement within our current system. I went through the entire design process from discovery to design with a little bit of testing sprinkled in.

Unfortunately, I can't show my final design work here, but don't hesitate to contact me to discuss my project!

Project Summary


My internship was scoped to focus on bringing trade partner interests into the design of our management tool but a solution can't be built without first knowing the problem. I was brought on to needfind and design accordingly with the full support of the company's technology and construction teams.

To date, this is the role that pushed me to grow the most as a designer. Not only was I working in tabular data for the first time but the efficiency required of this use case meant thinking critically about how the sequence that brings a user to a page affects what they see. For me, this brought new meaning to user centered design.

Takeaways


Know Your User
Mosaic's management tool has a few different types of users with varying permissions. It is important to make sure designs can be accessible by the target user with varying permissions across user groups. This means being intentional about who will be using this design. The next part is to know exactly what the user intends to accomplish. It helps to work backwards from here to clearly identify the parts of the user journey.

Learn How to Do Your Job Better
Feedback is a crucial step in strengthening design skills. What I loved most about working at Mosaic was the balance between independence and collaboration where both designers senior to me were open to talk through designs with me. These were valuable teachable moments that saved me so much time once I learned how to properly use auto layout. I'm embarrassed to tell you how I was designing tables before.

Keep Others Informed
Whenever you have the chance, communicate what you're working on to those outside of your immediate team - especially if your work affects them. This gives stakeholders an opportunity to share perspectives you may not have considered or share information you were unaware of. Since the world of construction and it's management was entirely new to me, this was especially necessary to ensure I was designing with my audience rather than for my audience.
