Mosaic Product Design


Mosaic is a Series B, tech-enabled general contractor for the residential development industry merging technology with construction services. I wore multiple hats accross design, research, and mangement over the course of my contract. While I was offered FTE, I made the decision to accept a scholarship to study Arabic while pursuing my masters degree.

Takeaways


Failure to Plan is a Plan to Fail
After working in tabular data for the first time, I realized my design process needed a facelift. Prioritizing user journeys and flow charts made all the difference. Mosaic's management tool has a few different types of users with varying permissions and goals when using the product. It was important to make sure interactions cater to the role of the user. Richard's advice: work backwards from a completed task to map how user goals affect their interactions and journey. My process for joining an existing product team has since adapted to begin by mapping my impression of the user's journey and discussing it with the person it represents.

Keep Others Informed
Whenever you have the chance, communicate what you're working on to those outside of your immediate team - especially if your work affects them. This gives stakeholders an opportunity to share perspectives you may not have considered or share information you were unaware of. Since the world of construction and it's management was entirely new to me, this was especially necessary to ensure I was designing with my audience rather than making assumptions on their behalf. I'm making it a habit to share simple recordings over Slack to keep communication open.

Co-work When You Can
What I loved most about working at Mosaic was the open door policy across the whole company. The designers on our team were always open to coworking after a feedback session. These turned out to be valuable teachable moments that saved me so much time once Chris showed me how he uses auto layout. I'm embarrassed to tell you how I was designing tables before. On the flip side, I was able to pay forward a Figma shortcut I learned from a peer from a previous organization. (If there are many layers of nesting, you can use cmnd + click to deep select a nested layer without needing to navigate to the layers panel)
