My 2018 summer internship was transformative for me as a systems thinker, user centered designer, and a team leader. My main project, the device list page, led me to think not only about the user journey leading into a design but how the experience fits into a larger system.

If you're interested in learning more about this project, let's talk.





What I Did





I worked on the digital, retail, and care team to support the redesign of AT&T's commercial site. Specifically, I worked with a scrum team to redesign a user's journey from browsing devices to purchase. This meant identifying whether the user was a new or returning customer, exploring how users would select a device, and easing them into a smooth checkout process.

Additionally, I led an intern project to integrate augmented reality to customer support calls to support first-time AT&T customers and joined the incubation of a user research team exploring travel plans for existing customers.

After work, you could catch playing for the company's softball team.









What I Learned





I began to see the "how" as an equally important measure as the "why." It tells a back story about the user that better helps the designer cater to their needs.

To do this, my manager helped me understand information architecture as a means to assess user intent. If the user were logged in, I'd have the data to indicate whether or not they were a returning customer, already owned a device, and had their payment information saved. Not only would this affect the content presented on the page - such as prices based on their phone plan or an option to trade in their old device - but also how long their checkout process would be.

Design isn't "one size fits all" and paying attention to what page a user is coming from can make the difference in where they are going.
